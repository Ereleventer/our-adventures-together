Featured Journey

Northern Italy & Switzerland

🇮🇹 🍕 🏔️  A 30th birthday trip · May 18 to 26, 2026

✦ Overview

Nine days of lakes, mountains and slow Italian afternoons — followed by a quiet Swiss escape and a final stretch on Lake Maggiore. We chased panoramic views, found our favourite gelato, and learned the rhythm of ferries between islands.

🗺️ The Route

  • 1 Lake Garda
  • 2 Lake Molveno
  • 3 Lake Lugano · Switzerland
  • 4 Lake Maggiore

💡 Tips We Learned

✈️ Flights

El Al felt like the safest option — and we had points from a cancelled flight to use.

📶 Internet

An unlimited 10‑day Voye plan worked beautifully (use code ALONBG for $5 off).

💳 Cash & Cards

You really don’t need cash anymore — but a little tucked away never hurts.

🛒 Supermarkets

Coop / Ipercoop and ALDI were our favourites. With a car, this becomes its own little adventure.

🚗 Car Rental

Skip the Telepass €20/day add‑on unless you’re truly using toll roads daily.

🍽️ At Restaurants

No tipping needed, and you pay at the register — not at the table.

🏞️ Lake Garda

Riva del Garda · Limone · Salò · Molveno

Where We Stayed

Hotel Benacus, Riva del Garda — three nights perched on a hillside, two minutes from the centre. Panoramic balconies, simple cosy rooms, and breakfast with the most ridiculous view.

Riva del Garda — Our Favourite

The lakeside square Piazza III Novembre became our happy place. Best meals: Leon D’Oro (the risotto), SUD (Neapolitan pizza), and Flora Gelateria for huge old‑school desserts.

Limone & Salò

Limone is a postcard — one long promenade, lemons everywhere, gelato at Roxy Bar. Salò is a beautiful, easy stop along the way.

Lake Molveno

An hour north — a quieter, fairy‑tale lake. The cable car climbs from an enclosed gondola to a wide‑open chairlift, with the Dolomites unfolding all around. A little terrifying, completely worth it.

🇨🇭 Lake Lugano

Switzerland · Cademario · Lugano · Locarno

Crossing Into Switzerland

An easy border — just remember the highway vignette at a gas station, and add cross‑border insurance to the rental car.

Where We Stayed

Kurhaus Cademario for two nights — a spa hotel up on the mountain, with a terrace view that doesn’t feel real and a beautifully large free spa.

Eating Around Lugano

Vittorio Vanini for gelato and chocolate, Pizza Al Volo for delivery (excellent), and Gusto Antico for huge pizza and arancini. Switzerland is expensive — and worth it.

To See

The Lugano promenade, the boats on the water, a quick stop in Locarno on the way back to Italy.

⛵ Lake Maggiore

Baveno · Stresa · The Borromean Islands

Where We Stayed

Grand Hotel Dino, Baveno — a resort‑style hotel with indoor and outdoor pools and a private beach right on the lake. Slightly old‑school, very lovely.

Baveno & Stresa

Baveno is small, sleepy and two minutes from the ferry. Golfo di Napoli served the best Neapolitan pizza of the trip. Stresa is the lively one — a long promenade with the islands floating just offshore, plus a swimming beach and the lovely Piazza Luigi Cardona.

The Borromean Islands

A €39 ferry pass for two, unlimited between islands. Isola Bella is all baroque palace and free‑roaming white peacocks. Isola dei Pescatori is the one for a long fish lunch — we ate at Imbarcadero, perfect.

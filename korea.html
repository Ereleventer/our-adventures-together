South Korea

🇰🇷 ⛩️  Two weeks of city, palaces & convenience stores · Sep 24 – Oct 7, 2025

✦ Overview

Two incredibly special weeks based out of Seoul, with day trips into the countryside. We wandered hanok villages, queued for dumplings at Gwangjang Market, ate too many pastries from convenience stores, and rode chairlifts through the Korean autumn.

💡 Tips We Learned

✈️ Flights

Etihad via Abu Dhabi — smooth and comfortable. On the 9‑hour layover, the duty‑free short‑stay hotel was a lifesaver (~$110 for 6 hours).

🏠 Where We Stayed

House of Rena Airbnb near Yaksu Station — 12 nights, fully equipped, washer + dryer, very safe building. Highly recommended.

📶 Internet

Voye eSIM, unlimited plan. Use code traveliri for ~15% off.

💳 Cash & Cards

Cards work almost everywhere — but markets usually need cash. Best exchange: K‑Exchange Myeongdong.

🚇 Transport

Excellent metro and buses. Use Naver Map (Google Maps doesn’t really work). Get a T‑Money card and tap in and out.

🚖 Taxis

Uber works well and is cheap. In Suwon and a few other areas, use K‑Ride instead.

🛬 Airport Transfer

Bus from the airport (~18,000 KRW) is easy and comfortable. Taxi back was around 55,000 KRW.

🧾 Tax Refund

Available on purchases over 15,000 KRW. Many stores do instant refunds with your passport — others reimburse at the airport.

🍱 Food & Habits

No tipping. Pay at the register, not the table. Most food is sweet or spicy — even when you expect it to be salty.

🏪 Convenience Stores (a love story)

7‑Eleven, CU, GS25, e‑mart — endless snacks, drinks and surprisingly soft bakery items. Our top tip: buy a cup of ice, then add any drink (or even water). You will not regret it.

🏙️ Seoul, Neighbourhood by Neighbourhood

We stayed only in Seoul and explored a different district almost every day.

🏡 Yaksu

Where we lived

A great area with everything close by — bakeries, cafes, restaurants, convenience stores.

Highlights

  • Yaksudong 103 — amazing bakery
  • Mega Coffee — cheap and HUGE drinks
  • Burger King, Papa John’s, Dunkin nearby
  • Olive Young (Korean beauty store)

🌃 Myeongdong

The bright, busy heart of Seoul

The Streets

The most central and touristy district — night market, bright streets, food stalls everywhere.

Food

From the street market: waffle coins with cream/cheese, mochi, skewers, tteokbokki, egg bread. For a sit‑down feast, Wangbijib served the best Korean BBQ of the trip — endless sides, perfect meat. 10/10.

Shopping

The best souvenir prices, plus Olive Young, All Mask Story, the 12‑floor Daiso, Pop Mart for collectibles, and NYUNYU for accessories.

🍵 Ikseon‑dong

Traditional houses, cute alleys, beautiful cafes

Food

  • Cheongsudong Café — magical stream‑themed design
  • Mil Toast — best French toast ever
  • Jayeondo Salt Bread — must try
  • Gaeseong Dumpling House (Michelin) — incredible dumplings

To See

Jogyesa Temple — a beautiful Buddhist temple, must visit.

🛍️ Seongsu

Shopping paradise & design heaven

Wildly creative stores, free gifts, raffles, and unique store designs everywhere.

Food

  • Cafe Onion
  • Bagel Land
  • Shake Shack
  • Paper Plate (great NY‑style pizza)

Shopping

  • Gentle Monster (a wild design experience)
  • Adidas customisation
  • NYUNYU

🏯 Jongno

Palaces, hanok houses & Gwangjang Market

Highlights

  • London Bagel Museum — the famous bakery
  • Gwangjang Market — the best dumplings & street food
  • Bukchon Hanok Village — traditional houses on a hillside

🌆 Gangnam

COEX, Starfield Library & the famous statue

Highlights

Food

Taco Booth NYC, Wingstop, plus great Japanese spots.

🎤 Hongdae

Young, lively, full of street performances

Food

Jangin Dakgalbi — a wonderful spicy chicken dish, very highly recommended.

⛰️ Eunpyeong Hanok Village

The most beautiful place we visited

Quiet, peaceful, surrounded by mountains, far less touristy than the central districts.

Food

1in1jan Café — incredible views and great food.

Highlights

  • Walking the village itself
  • Jingwansa Temple — a hidden gem

🎢 Lotte World

Honestly? Not worth it — long lines, overrated. Skip it.

The Philippines

🇵🇭 🌴 🏝️  The post‑army adventure · May 28 – June 24

✦ Overview

Almost a month of island‑hopping, scooter rides and tropical sunsets — a post‑army trip across some of the most beautiful islands on earth. We stayed in lovely hotels, mostly flew between destinations, and didn’t hold back on food or experiences.

Budget: ~₪16,500 for two — including international flights, domestic flights, accommodation, food and attractions. Booked roughly two months in advance.

🗺️ The Route

  • 1 Manila
  • 2 Coron
  • 3 El Nido
  • 4 Port Barton
  • 5 Boracay
  • 6 Cebu
  • 7 Bohol
  • 8 Siargao

💡 Tips We Learned

💸 Don't Overthink Money

Once you've decided to go — go. It would be a shame to miss experiences for the sake of budget.

💊 Malaria Pills

Only needed for Palawan. Start 1–2 days before arrival, continue a few days after leaving.

🦟 Mosquitoes

So many. Use Off! repellent constantly — especially around dawn and dusk.

✈️ Transfers

Be mentally ready for long, sometimes tough travel between destinations. Things go wrong — go with the flow.

🛬 Arrival Tasks (Manila Airport)

  • SIM card: Smart — 16GB + 30 local mins + 10 international mins for 1500 PHP. Essential.
  • Currency exchange: Metro Bank (~52 PHP = $1). Keep some USD as backup.
  • Grab to hotel: Way better than taxis — cheaper and easier pickup.

🏙️ Manila

2 nights · jet‑lag recovery

Huge, busy and not particularly interesting — we stayed mostly to recover from jet lag. Use Grab to get around (avoid rush hour).

Where We Stayed

Lub D Makati — nice lobby, rooftop and restaurant. The room itself was OK but not perfectly clean.

Food

Plenty of familiar chains around (McDonald’s, Starbucks…) plus a fun night street‑food market.

Attractions

  • I’m Onsen Spa — great experience (~1900 PHP)
  • Dessert Museum — surprisingly fun
  • Mall of Asia — gigantic

🏝️ Coron

3 nights · island‑hopping base

Small town, mainly a base for island hopping — no real swimming beach in town itself. Get around by tricycle (very cheap).

Where We Stayed

  • Princess of Coron — basic, not recommended
  • Soleil Garden Resort — amazing, worth every peso

Food

  • Altrove — Italian
  • Buzz — varied menu

Attractions

  • Private tour with Jonathan — highly recommended. Snorkelling, beautiful spots, tailored to us. 📞 +63 966 183 2298
  • Hot Springs — nice, but go at sunset to avoid the crowds

🌊 El Nido

7 nights · one of the best stops of the trip

One of the absolute highlights — endless beaches, tours and pockets to explore. Tricycle gets you everywhere.

Where We Stayed

  • Lagun — great central hotel
  • Seda Lio — luxury, expensive, outstanding
  • Sea Cocoon — good location but strict policies

Food

Altrove, Taste (vegan), Gusto Gelato, El Nido Grill, Globy.

Attractions

  • Lio Beach — the best beach. Highly recommended.
  • Las Cabanas — chill beach with a zipline
  • Organic Spa — excellent
  • Tour C — nice, though could be better planned

🥞 Port Barton

3 nights · quiet, magical, amazing food

A quiet, small town with the loveliest vibe. Mostly walkable.

Where We Stayed

Oasis — a great little guesthouse.

Food

  • Gorgonzola — best pizza in the Philippines
  • Reef Cafe — great burgers
  • Besaga — famous banana Nutella pancakes

Attractions

  • Boat tour with Edward — incredible snorkelling
  • White Beach — the only proper swimming beach nearby

🏖️ Boracay

4 nights · touristy, but worth it

Very touristy — a bit Eilat‑in‑the‑tropics. Still worth a stop.

Where We Stayed

  • 8 Colors — not recommended
  • Fairways & Bluewater — incredible resort, great value

Food

  • Aria — Italian
  • Sunny Side Cafe — amazing breakfast
  • Rima — fine dining

Attractions

  • D’Mall — main strip
  • Parasailing & helmet diving — guide Julie 📞 +63 906 047 9147
  • Diniwid Beach — quieter side

🌃 Cebu

1 night · transit stop

Modern city but not much to do — a transit point only. Our flights got delayed and we lost a night in Bohol because of it.

Where We Stayed

Red Planet — great for a short overnight.

🌳 Bohol

2 nights · Chocolate Hills & tarsiers

A compact island with surprisingly diverse land tours.

Where We Stayed

Alona Pawikan — recommended.

Food

  • Shaka — great vegan food
  • Noah Pizza

Attractions

  • Alona Beach
  • Day tour — Chocolate Hills, ATV ride, tarsiers, butterfly farm. Driver: Ramel 📞 +63 948 617 9890

🏄 Siargao

5 nights · our favourite island

The most relaxed, magical place we visited. Get a scooter or golf cart — it makes the whole island yours.

Where We Stayed

RGL — great spot, very central.

Food

  • Kermit
  • Bravo — pool + beach view
  • Green House — best meal of the entire trip
  • Bake — great breakfasts

Attractions

  • Cloud 9 — the famous surfing beach
  • Secret Beach — quiet and beautiful
  • Coconut Viewpoint — stunning
  • Magpupungko Rock Pools

⭐ Final Notes

  • The Philippines has incredible diversity between its islands
  • Expect travel challenges — and unforgettable experiences
  • Siargao and El Nido were the highlights
  • Port Barton is perfect for relaxing
  • Boracay brings a completely different, modern vibe
